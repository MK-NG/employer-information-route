Industry placements: T Levels

T levels are a qualification for students aged 16 to 19 who have finished GCSEs. They are a 2-year qualification and the equivalent to 3 A levels.

They have been designed with employers to help young people develop the knowledge, attitude and practical skills to thrive in the workplace.

Employer costs

No cost but employers may choose to pay the wages and any payments for expenses such as travel and living costs.

Employer responsibility

Employers must provide:

  • a safe work environment
  • opportunities for the student to develop their technical skills within your industry
  • a line manager to support, supervise and mentor the student
  • an induction which includes explaining relevant policies and procedures
  • formal feedback on the studentâ€™s progress against the agreed learning goals at the end of the placement

Benefits of T Levels

T-levels are available in more than 20 courses, covering 11 skill areas.

These include:

  • agriculture, environmental and animal care
  • business and administration
  • catering and hospitality
  • creative and design
  • digital and IT
  • education and childcare
  • engineering and manufacturing
  • hair and beauty
  • health and science
  • legal, finance and accounting
